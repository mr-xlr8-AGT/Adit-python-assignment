Introduction: The Recurring Specter of Financial Collapse

Financial markets have demonstrated a remarkable tendency toward periodic crises separated by intervals of 6-10 years. Between 2000 and 2025, the global financial system has experienced three major asset bubbles, each preceded by similar warning signs and followed by widespread destruction of capital, employment, and investor confidence.

Corrections to Previous Analysis

Housing Prices (2008 Crisis)

Original: tripled

Corrected: approximately doubled (100% appreciation) nationally, with certain regional markets (Florida, Arizona, Nevada) experiencing 150-200% appreciation

Moody's AAA Ratings (2008 Crisis)

Original: 87% of total volume as AAA

Corrected: Of mortgage-backed securities that were later downgraded, approximately 30% carried AAA ratings at issuance. During peak securitization periods (2005-2007), 60-80% of newly issued tranches received AAA ratings.

AI Sector Cash Burn (2025)

Original: $20+ billion/year

Corrected: Aggregate AI sector cash burn estimated at $12-18 billion annually across major players: OpenAI (~$5B), Anthropic (~$10B), Meta AI infrastructure (~$4B), with significant variation by company
